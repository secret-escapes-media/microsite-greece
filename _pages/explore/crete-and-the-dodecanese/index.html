This translates to glorious old towns, clashes of culture that play themselves out in the flavour of each island, and typical Greek charms. Discover these alluring islands in our guide. sections: - id: crete type: large title: Crete description: The Cretan landscape is framed by wild and rugged peaks to the south, a patchwork of mountain villages, ancient harbour towns containing a hive of activity, and stunning coastlines of soft sands lapped by emerald waters. A land so diverse among the Mediterranean, Crete is a standalone outpost of unique culture expressed in its food and drink, and a fervent identity shadowed by a history and heritage that imbues pride amongst the people of the island. A big island with a bigger heart, you’ll find treasures like the sublime Samaria Gorge and a range of tavernas supplied by local produce, meaning epic scenery and addictive dining. From bustling cities to laid-back coastal towns, Crete caters to a wide range of tastes, always offering its unique style and taste to the delight of travellers. pois: - title: Rethymno id: rethymno description: Labyrinthine streets are shrouded by canopies of flora, pierced by Ottoman minarets and Venetian balconies. Rethymno hides fine restaurants, bars and boutiques in narrow alleyways beset with effortless charm. A student population ensures a network of cool bars hosting live music, that spill out onto the streets after sunset. A true gem of a town. - title: Agios Nikolaos id: agios-nikolaos description: A stylish town built around an enchanting lagoon, Agios Nikolaos has long been a firm favourite of chic holiday makers. With luxury boutiques contending with wholesome tavernas and plenty of bars, there’s no shortage of activity from day, well into the night. Dine right next to the water, under the lights of the bars and restaurants that fringe the lagoon. - title: Pink Beaches id: pink-beaches description: At the western tip of Crete lie two astounding beaches. Balos and Elafonisi both perch next to lagoons that offer warm dips in remarkably clear waters. Yet the true marvel comes from the sublime pink hues that both beaches offer – a technicolour wonder, stupendously rare, that has to be seen to be believed. - title: Minoan Heritage id: minoan-heritage description: Crete was the home of the first civilization on European soil – the Minoans. Their history is dotted across the island in palaces and settlements that are startlingly ancient. From the marvel of Knossos, where myths of the minotaur still reverberate around the palace walls, to the remarkable palace at Phaistos, the Minoans aren’t hard to find on this island. - title: Balos id: balos description: A sublime beauty spot, Balos is an isolated lagoon beach where shallow, translucent waters wash over soft white sands. To reach it, a boat excursion or a trip in a 4x4 along dusty paths is necessary. The journey is worth it – the twisting lagoon makes for a perfect day of beach lounging in idyllic surroundings. - title: Iraklio Wine Country id: iraklio-wine-country description: This famed wine region offers a cluster of wine estates, where Cretan grape varieties produce tasty tipples that are slowly being recognised outside of Crete for their refined and sumptuous qualities. Hand in hand with these famed offerings stand an irresistible assortment of restaurants that make for an itinerary of mouth-watering quality. - id: rhodes type: large title: Rhodes description: The largest of the Dodecanese, Rhodes marries a rich history with buzzing towns, resplendent coastline, isolated wooded forests and simple Greek charms from top to bottom. Whether you’re going for endless sun lounging on crisp, white sands, or you prefer exploring Byzantine cities and imposing Knights’ fortresses, Rhodes is an inspired destination that caters to many tastes. Labyrinthine old towns lead to quiet rural villages, where rich food and drink abound in the family tavernas and locals put the world to rights in buzzing bars. Generations of different cultures have mixed in this strategic location, with Turkish, Jewish and Greek melding to carve out a unique identity in the Dodecanese. Discover this cultural menagerie in the architecture, delicacies and flavours of the island. pois: - title: Rhodes Old Town id: rhodes-old-town description: It’s the largest medieval city in Europe, a famed UNESCO heritage site, and in a word, stunning. Navigate narrow Byzantine alleyways and discover boutiques, restaurants and bars that spill out onto squares filled with dreams of knights and crusades. Walk the ancient walls, or discover sublime churches and breathtaking palaces. A true historical wonderland. - title: Lindos id: lindos description: Take a boat trip to Lindos, a small town with an abundant charm. From the picturesque beach, the colourful houses wrap around the grand Acropolis, crowned by a fortification and ancient temple plus breathtaking views. A warren of narrow streets reveal architectural clues of shifting time – find Turkish, Frankish, Byzantine and much older all represented here. Cafes and tavernas hide in the patchwork of cobbled lanes, as you dodge donkeys to find one of many idyllic resting spots. - title: Tsambika Monastery id: tsambika-monastery description: Explore this famed pilgrimage site for simply stunning views over the coastline. The Panagia Tsambika Monastery draws female pilgrims, particularly during festivals, to pray for fertility – some of whom will crawl to the top on their hands and knees. The 298 steps offer simply jaw-dropping views crowned by a beautiful 17th century church with incredible fragments of frescoes. After the heady climb, relax on one of the most beautiful beaches in Rhodes. - title: Valley of the Butterflies id: valley-of-the-butterflies description: A ravishing beauty spot lies in the hills just south of Rhodes Town, for inspired outdoor adventuring. This enchanting place, sheltered by trees and dotted with rivers and waterfalls, attracts a colourful collection of butterflies from June until September. A truly magical outing, don’t miss this one. - id: kos type: medium title: Kos subtitle: An Island Of Two Halves description: This second largest of the Dodecanese is a fiery mix of ancient history, stunning beaches, buzzing nightlife and effortless relaxation. A landscape of two extremes, the majority of the island is flat, making it perfect for long cycle rides exploring quiet villages. The south rises dramatically, offering beaches with grand mountainous backdrops and secluded mountain villages with great local delicacies. Kos Town, like the rest of the island, is littered with ancient finds. Corinthian columns rise out of the urban scene, rubbing shoulders with Roman mosaics and Venetian Knights’ castles. Italianate buildings and public gardens are pierced my minarets, adding to the heady concoction of culture on display. Kos caters to many tastes – enjoy the nightlife of Kos Town or find your own quiet island retreat on the rest of the land. - id: symi type: small title: Symi description: Those that arrive to Symi by ferry will be in awe of the beauty of the waterfront buildings. A colourful menagerie of Italianate mansions of burnt orange and sandy hues create a dazzling display of beauty to any visitor that arrives by sea. The rest of the island is pretty barren, but gorgeous beaches are to be found everywhere. A truly beautiful destination that should be more than just a day trip from Rhodes. - id: nisyros type: small title: Nisyros description: An island of wild beauty, Nisyros offers a volcanic landscape and remote island charms. Literally the tip of a volcano, this small outcrop matches dramatic natural features like the grand caldera with a wash of charming white buildings hiding tavernas and bars. For secluded beauty, epic hikes and peaceful harbour life, stop by. - id: kalymnos type: small title: Kalymnos description: Kos’s neighbour is a mountainous treasure that offers quiet, relaxed Greek rural scenes that draws outdoor enthusiasts and those looking for true peace on holiday. The working hub of Pothia isn’t particularly touristy, yet the island’s true delights lie in the quiet villages to the south. For private beaches, quiet towns and local delicacies like squid in ouzo, Kalymnos makes a great stop. ---
